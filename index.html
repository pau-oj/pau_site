Ladrilludo

Walking the Public Pedagogical Pulse through Bogot√°‚Äôs Urban Fabric.

üìÑ READ FULL RESEARCH PAPER

Ladrilludo reimagines a ubiquitous building material as an "itinerant, sensing object". By removing a clay brick from its static context and dragging it through the streets, the object transforms into a "stylus"‚Äîa sensitive indicator tracing the topography and registering the frequencies of the city's surface.

Grounded in Pluriversal Thinking and Object-Oriented Inquiry (OOI), this performance actively interrogates the "smooth" narratives of urban renewal. The brick, tethered like a pet, became a "dialogue artifact", provoking spontaneous interactions (mamadera de gallo) and uncovering the silenced industrial history of the Las Aguas neighborhood.

// SOCIOPOLITICAL CONTEXT

The origin of this inquiry is rooted in the recent social unrest in Colombia (the "Paro" or national strike). As I watched my country fracture under the weight of systemic inequality, the "white cube" of the art gallery felt suffocating. This project moves the inquiry to the street, where the friction is real. The brick is not just matter; it is a witness to the "invisible codes" of class stratification.

Fig 1. The brick as an itinerant participant on the cobbled streets of Bogot√°.

The transformation from debris to device involved drilling the ceramic body to embed a lavalier microphone deep within its core.

02. RUPTURE & REPAIR

Fig 3. The brick split mid-performance and was repaired with duct tape.

"The brick wears down, breaks, and is patched."

Midway through the walk, the interface failed. The brick split under the physical stress of the city. Instead of replacing the object, we engaged in a ritual of repair using duct tape. This moment of rupture became central to the inquiry.

Doll House

Unfolding memory: An analog inquiry into the "scripted" nature of girlhood.

"We are taught that being feminine means following certain paths... The dollhouse is both a symbol of innocence and a miniature cage."

Doll House is a photographic object that explores the construction of identity through analog interactivity. Taking the form of a childhood pop-up book, the piece invites the viewer to physically "open" and "reveal" the domestic spaces where gender roles are learned.

Unlike digital interfaces that alienate the hand, this project demands tactile engagement. The viewer must manipulate the paper mechanisms to uncover the images, mimicking the way we selectively unpack (or repress) our own childhood memories. It is a low-tech interface for high-resolution emotional data.

Fig 1. The artist performing the role of the "living doll" within the constructed narrative.

The Aesthetic of "Cute"

The piece utilizes the aesthetic of kawaii or "cuteness" (pastels, miniatures, soft textures) as a critical camouflage.

By inviting the viewer to play with these seemingly innocent objects, the work lowers their defenses, allowing the underlying critique of gender conditioning ("the miniature cage") to enter the subconscious. It is a "Trojan Horse" made of cardstock and memories.

Hu√©sped Errante

Programmable decay: A thermodynamic visualization of obsession as a viral agent.

"Obsession creeps in, ruminating silently, contaminating every corner of the being until it cools and fades."

Hu√©sped Errante (Wandering Guest) is a living sculpture that investigates the physical manifestation of intrusive thoughts. Moving beyond digital screens, I sought to create an interface that behaves like an organism.

Using the principles of thermodynamics, the piece features a chemically responsive skin that "breathes" in cycles of heat and cold. [cite_start]It visualizes the behavior of a parasite: it remains dormant and invisible until a trigger (heat) causes it to manifest, bleaching the surface in mutating biological patterns before fading back into the subconscious grey[cite: 69].

The challenge was not just changing color, but controlling the hysteresis‚Äîthe lag between the heating input and the cooling output. This required a custom circuit design that could push 110V through nichrome resistors without incinerating the delicate pigment layer.

02. The Protocol of Decay

Unlike a digital pixel which turns on and off instantly, this material has memory. The heat dissipates slowly, meaning the "image" lingers like a ghost.

I programmed the Arduino not as a linear loop, but as a stochastic system that mimics viral replication. The resistors activate in sequences that appear random but follow a logic of "infection," heating adjacent zones to create a spreading effect that looks organic rather than mechanical.

La Soledad Suena

Re-materializing the void: A reactive audiovisual environment driven by body presence.

"Loneliness is not silence; it is a high-density frequency."

La Soledad Suena is an immersive interface that translates the poetic structure of loneliness into a physical, reactive space. Unlike static installations, this system uses computer vision to acknowledge the viewer's presence.

I engineered a real-time feedback loop where the visitor's body data (captured via depth sensors) directly modulates the audiovisual environment. The silence of the room is deceptive; as the body moves, it triggers layers of "domestic noise"‚Äîthe hum of a fridge, the friction of fabric‚Äîforcing the user to physically inhabit the soundscape of isolation.

Fig 1. The immersive threshold.

01. SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

The installation operates on a custom-built node network in TouchDesigner. A Kinect sensor captures the depth map of the room, isolating the visitor from the background. This data is mapped to the opacity and distortion parameters of the visual projection, creating a "ghostly" latency between the person and their digital shadow.

Traces of the Imperceptible

A 24-hour generative sonic topography that remembers the bodies that pass through it.

"The soundscape feels populated, even when the space is empty."

Traces of the Imperceptible transforms a transit area into a spatial instrument of memory. Using over 1,500 meters of tensioned rope, we materialized the invisible trajectories of daily life.

My contribution was the "invisible architecture": a generative audio system that doesn't just react to presence, but accumulates it. I engineered a sensor network that captures the passage of bodies and translates them into persistent sonic layers. The result is a "ghostly density"‚Äîa feeling of being accompanied by the resonance of those who have already left.

Fig 1. 1,500 meters of rope creating a physical glitch in the architectural grid.

01. ENGINEERING PRESENCE

The core logic is additive, not substitutive. The algorithm treats every visitor as a signal that is added to the loop, increasing the complexity of the composition over time.

INPUT

Motion Sensors
Triggering sonic accumulation.

ALGORITHM

Spectral Density
Layering sounds to simulate crowds.

DURATION

24-Hour Cycle
Non-repetitive generative evolution.

Fig 2. "Ordered Disorder": Analyzing the chaotic path of human transit.

Sonic Afterimage

I programmed the system to function as an auditory memory bank. When a sensor is triggered, a specific sound file is added to the active composition. These sounds do not fade instantly; they linger, stacking upon one another.

This creates a "phantom crowd" effect. A solitary person walking the stairs hears the sonic footsteps and whispers of the dozens who passed before them, blurring the line between current presence and past trajectory.
