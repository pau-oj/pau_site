Ladrilludo

Role: Co-Researcher
Materials: Brick, Mic, Arduino
Context: Public Space / Bogotá
[ Foto Principal: El Ladrillo en la Calle ]

This project begins with a question: How does inequality sound?


By embedding acoustic sensors into a standard construction brick, I created a device that records the "friction" of the city. Dragging this object through different neighborhoods of Bogotá generated a sonic map of social stratification.


It acts as an acoustic probe disguised as debris. The result is not just data, but a physical performance of labor and resistance.

La Soledad Suena

Role: Interaction Designer
Software: TouchDesigner
Output: Immersive Installation
[ Foto: Proyección Inmersiva ]

An investigation into the "silent phenomenology" of isolation.


Using TouchDesigner, this installation creates a real-time feedback loop. It visualizes the silence of the room and the subtle movements of the spectator, translating solitude into a dense, immersive sonic environment.

Huésped Errante

Role: Prototyper
Tech: Thermochromics / Heating
Concept: Organic Chaos
[ Foto: Detalle de los Pigmentos Cambiando ]

Can rigid engineering breathe life into organic matter?


"Huésped Errante" is a living sculpture. Hidden circuits control heating elements that activate thermosensitive pigments on the surface. The result is a mutating biological pattern that shifts and changes, mimicking the unpredictable behavior of a living organism.
