METHOD Object-Oriented Inquiry

Ladrilludo

Can a construction brick become a recording interface for urban inequality?

Ladrilludo transforms a found brick into a sensing object. Drilled to accommodate microphones and tethered for walking, the brick becomes an "itinerant participant" recording the raw textures of Bogotá [cite: 216-217].

The project gives physical weight to the concept of social mobility. By dragging this debris through the city, particularly the industrial "Las Aguas" neighborhood, we record the friction of displacement, turning invisible history into an audible soundscape [cite: 222-223].

Filtering mechanical noise from the tethering friction.

02. PERFORMING FRICTION

Walking-with the brick functioned as a method of "sensory attunement". The friction of the clay against the pavement generated a sonic map of the city's surfaces—distinguishing the smooth tiles of gentrified zones from the rough concrete of working-class streets[cite: 220].

This act provoked "spontaneous interactions" with passersby, using humor and curiosity to open dialogues about urban erasure and the labor of carrying history[cite: 221].
